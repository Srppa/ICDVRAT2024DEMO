15th International Conference on Disability, Virtual Reality & Associated Technologies

From the Florence Bus Station

We recommend taking the metro line C, situated directly at the main train station. Take direction „Háje“, to the station „Muzeum“ and changing to metro line A, direction: “Nemocnice Motol“, to the station „Dejvická“. The conference hall is just few steps walking from Dejvicka station.

Public transport in Prague, including bus, tram and subway are subject to regular Prague Public Transport Fares (including pre-paid passes). The passengers must buy a ticket before onboarding and validate them once entered the subway station or bus or tram. Tickets are sold in the Visitor Centre, at the Prague Public Transit company counters in Arrival Halls, or from ticket vending machines at metro station. Most ticket machines accept contactless credit card payments. Tickets can also be purchased via SMS or the Lítačka mobile app, which can be also used to search for the ideal public transport connection. The application is available for Android and iOS.

You can also choose some of the taxi services in Prague, or services like Uber, Bolt or Liftago. It takes approximately 20-30 minutes of ride from the airport to the conference hall (Czech Institute of Informatics, Robotics and Cybernetics, Jugoslávských partyzánů 1580/3, Prague 6, Dejvice)
